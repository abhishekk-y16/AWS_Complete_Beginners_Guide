Cloud computing means using someone else's computers over the internet instead of owning your own.

## Simple Analogy: The Electricity Model

**Before (1900s):**
- Every factory had its own power generator
- Expensive to buy ($50,000+)
- Needed engineers to maintain
- Wasted when factory closed

**After (Today):**
- Plug into power grid
- Pay for what you use
- Power company maintains it
- No waste

**Cloud Computing = Same idea for computers!**

## Traditional vs Cloud

### Traditional IT (Old Way)
```
Buy Physical Servers:
- Upfront: $50,000
- Wait: 3-6 months
- Data center: $5,000/month
- IT staff: $300,000/year
- Maintenance: $10,000/year

Total Year 1: $450,000+
Utilization: 20% (80% wasted!)
```

### Cloud Computing (New Way)
```
Rent Virtual Servers:
- Upfront: $0
- Wait: 5 minutes
- No data center
- No IT staff (for basics)
- No maintenance

Total Year 1: $1,200-60,000
Utilization: 90% (efficient!)
```

## Types of Cloud Services

### 1. IaaS (Infrastructure as a Service)
**Like:** Renting a car
- You drive and maintain it
- Full control

**AWS Examples:**
- EC2 (servers)
- S3 (storage)
- VPC (networking)

**Good for:** Custom apps, full control

### 2. PaaS (Platform as a Service)
**Like:** Taking Uber
- Driver handles maintenance
- Less control, more convenience

**AWS Examples:**
- Elastic Beanstalk
- RDS (managed database)
- Lambda (serverless)

**Good for:** Focus on code, not infrastructure

### 3. SaaS (Software as a Service)
**Like:** Taking the bus
- Fixed route
- Least control, most convenient

**Examples:**
- Gmail
- Salesforce
- Dropbox

**Good for:** Standard business apps
